EXPAND

FIRST-PERSON 3D PLATFORMER

DESCRIPTION

Developed for the GMTK Jam 2026 under the theme "Build to Scale", Expand is a 3D first-person puzzle-platformer created in just three days. Our team of four consisted of a pixel artist for the bow, my friend Jan-Peter for the audio and two designers. I was part of the design team but focused primarily on the programming. The original concept featured a bow that could throw gizmos onto any object to rotate, translate, or scale them to solve puzzles. Due to the tight deadline, we narrowed the scope to scaling arrows that expand the faces of specific cubes. During development, we discovered that scaling a face near the player provided a significant velocity boost, we decided to lean into this mechanic, shifting the game’s focus from a traditional puzzle game toward a high-mobility platformer.
